# 11 — Mobile Vision

GIOK on the desktop is the cockpit. GIOK on mobile is **presence everywhere** — because life
doesn't happen at a desk. The gym, the client's kitchen table, the school pickup line, the
booth at the fair: that's where the most valuable captures and the most important reminders
occur. Mobile is not a shrunken desktop; it is the **capture-and-awareness** half of GIOK.

**Mobile design north star:** *can Jake capture a thought or know the next thing in one hand, in
three seconds, without breaking eye contact with a person?*

## Quick Capture (the reason mobile exists)
- The flagship mobile capability. One tap from anywhere — lock screen, widget, share sheet — to
  dump a thought.
- **Zero friction:** capture first, organize never-at-capture-time. Tony routes it later.
- Works **offline**; syncs when back online. A thought is never lost to bad signal.
- Voice-first option so Jake can capture hands-free and eyes-up.

## Notifications
- The right nudge at the right moment: a Checkup due today, a client reply, a non-negotiable at
  risk, an alert from Mission Control.
- **Ranked by consequence, honest, and quiet by default** — GIOK never spams. Notifications
  respect focus; they inform, they don't nag.
- Actionable from the notification itself where safe (mark done, snooze, add to today).

## Mission Control (mobile)
- A phone-shaped situation glance: the vital widgets — priorities, alerts, agent health, business
  and health pulse — readable at a stoplight.
- The dense desktop grid re-flows to a prioritized vertical stack; same data, mobile-first order.

## Widgets
- **Home-screen widgets** for the highest-frequency needs: one-tap Quick Capture, Today's
  Priorities, Life/Business Score, next appointment.
- Widgets make GIOK *ambient* — value without opening the app.

## Voice
- Capture by voice, and eventually **converse with Tony** by voice — ask what's next, log a win,
  add a task, get the morning brief read aloud on the drive.
- Voice is the natural interface for a chief of staff you talk to, not type at.

## Camera
- Snap a receipt, a whiteboard, a business card, a form — the image is captured and its text
  extracted, then routed (financial, business, home, learning).
- The camera turns the physical world into capturable input.

## Offline support
- **Local-first:** captures and views work without a connection; sync reconciles later.
- Non-negotiable for a capture tool — friction from a spinner is friction that loses the thought.
- Conflict resolution is honest and safe: nothing is silently overwritten.

## What mobile is NOT (for now)
- Not the place for deep editing, configuration, or long-form work — those stay on desktop.
- Not a second source of truth — mobile reads and writes the same synced truth, never a fork.

Mobile's promise: wherever Jake is, GIOK can catch the thought and tell him the next right
thing — one-handed, eyes-up, present with the people in front of him.
