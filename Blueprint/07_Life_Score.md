# 07 — Life Score

The Life Score is how GIOK turns "how is life actually going?" into an honest, glanceable signal —
and, over time, into coaching. It exists because what gets measured gets managed, and because a
disciplined operator deserves a scoreboard for the *whole* life, not just the business.

The Life Score must always obey the honesty clause: **a score is never inflated to feel good.**
An honest low score that prompts a real change is worth more than a flattering fake one.

## Categories

The Life Score is a composite of category scores, each 0–100:

| Category | What it reflects |
|----------|------------------|
| **Business** | Agency momentum — pipeline health, Checkups completed, referrals, revenue activity |
| **Health** | Training, sleep, nutrition, recovery, medical upkeep |
| **Financial** | Budget adherence, savings/targets, obligations met, runway |
| **Family** | Presence, promises kept, time invested, connection |
| **Learning** | Deliberate growth — reading, skills, study, ideas developed |
| **Relationships** | Beyond family — friends, mentors, network, clients as people |
| **Consistency** | Non-negotiables and streaks — did the disciplines actually happen |
| **Home** | The physical life — house, projects, maintenance, order |
| **Overall** | The weighted composite — the single "how's life" number |

## How scores work
- Each category rolls up from concrete signals in its workspace (habits done, tasks completed on
  time, commitments kept, metrics logged). No signal → the score is honestly marked
  *insufficient data*, not guessed.
- **Overall** is a weighted blend. Weights are configurable and reflect Jake's priorities (e.g.
  Consistency and Family may carry extra weight because they're load-bearing for everything else).
- Scores are **directional, not clinical.** The point is momentum and awareness, not false
  precision. A score is a conversation starter, not a verdict.
- **Consistency** is special: it measures whether the non-negotiables actually happened, because
  discipline compounds and props up every other category.

## How trends work
- Every category and the overall carry a **trend** (up / flat / down) over rolling windows (week,
  month, quarter). The trend often matters more than the absolute number — GIOK celebrates
  *direction*.
- Trends drive the narrative on Home and Mission Control: "Health up three weeks running,"
  "Consistency slipping — two non-negotiables missed this week."
- Trends are honest about regression. A dropping score is surfaced early and kindly, so it can be
  corrected before it becomes a hole.

## Future metrics
As live integrations arrive, categories deepen with real data:
- **Health:** wearables (steps, sleep, HRV, workouts).
- **Financial:** account balances (read-only), commission tracking, budget tools.
- **Business:** CRM pipeline stages, Checkup completion rates, referral counts.
- **Consistency:** automatic habit detection from captures and calendar.
- **Relationships:** last-contact recency for key people (nudge before someone goes cold).

## The Life Score and the improvement cycle
The Life Score is the scoreboard for the **Plan → Execute → Audit → Improve** loop (see
[Continuous_Improvement.md](Continuous_Improvement.md)):
- The **Morning Briefing** opens with a **Life Score / Business Score snapshot** — where things
  stand before the day begins (Plan).
- The **End of Day Audit** produces an honest **Day Score** across the same categories (Overall,
  Business, Health, Financial, Family, Consistency, Learning, Relationship), which rolls up into
  the trend (Audit).
- Those trends drive the next day's plan and Tony's coaching (Improve).
- **Consistency** is scored from the non-negotiable streaks captured in the daily audit — the
  most direct link between the daily ritual and the long-term score.

Day scores are honest and never inflated to feel good; the audit's job is to make Jake better,
not to flatter him.

## Future coaching
The Life Score is the substrate for Tony's coaching:
- **Weekly review:** Tony walks the scores, names the wins, flags the one category to focus on,
  and proposes a single concrete move — not ten.
- **Early warning:** Tony catches a downslope before it's a crisis ("Family time down two weeks —
  block Thursday?").
- **Balance guard:** if Business climbs while Family and Health fall, Tony says so — *People
  Matter More Than Money* enforced by the scoreboard itself.
- **Goal linkage:** category trends connect to Goals, showing whether daily behavior is actually
  moving the year's intentions.

The Life Score's job is not to judge Jake. It's to give him — and Tony — an honest, shared view
of the whole life, so the right small correction happens at the right time.
